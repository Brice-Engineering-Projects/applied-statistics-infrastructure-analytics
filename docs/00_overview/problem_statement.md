# Hydrology Probability Exercise

## Background

You are a hydrologic engineer analyzing annual maximum streamflow data collected over a 20-year period at a stream gaging station.

Your objective is to understand how probability distributions describe observed data and how engineers use cumulative probabilities to evaluate design risk.

This exercise intentionally uses a small dataset so that calculations can be verified manually if desired.

---

# Objectives

By completing this exercise, you should understand:

- Histograms
- Probability Distribution Functions (PDF)
- Cumulative Distribution Functions (CDF)
- Exceedance probability
- Basic engineering interpretation

---

# Tasks

## Part 1

Load the dataset.

Determine:

- Number of observations
- Mean
- Median
- Minimum
- Maximum
- Standard deviation

---

## Part 2

Create a histogram.

Answer:

- Where do most observations occur?
- Is the distribution symmetric?
- Is it skewed?

---

## Part 3

Estimate the Probability Distribution Function (PDF).

Plot the PDF over the histogram.

Answer:

- What range of flows appears most likely?
- Which flows appear least likely?

---

## Part 4

Construct the Cumulative Distribution Function (CDF).

Plot cumulative probability versus streamflow.

Answer:

- What proportion of annual peak flows are less than 600 cfs?
- What proportion are less than 700 cfs?
- What proportion exceed 700 cfs?
- What proportion exceed 800 cfs?

---

## Part 5

Engineering Interpretation

A proposed culvert has a hydraulic capacity of 700 cfs.

Using your CDF, estimate:

- Probability that annual peak flow is less than or equal to the culvert capacity.
- Probability that annual peak flow exceeds the culvert capacity.

Discuss whether this appears to be an acceptable design based solely on the observed record.

---

## Bonus

Without changing the dataset:

Compare the histogram,
the PDF,
and the CDF.

Describe in your own words what unique information each provides.

Imagine explaining the difference to another engineer with no formal statistics background.
